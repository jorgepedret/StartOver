    • A Seed Or A Weed?

      A seed or a weed? It is a matter of perspective...

      and purpose.

      Dandelions have taken over the world because they are perfectly designed.

      Consider this: Every part of the dandelion is edible: roots, leaves, and flowers.

      Yes they are an invasive species... but they are tasty and nutritious! Why take them out?

      The dandelion has been used as a medicine longer than written history.

      The plant is useful to humans, animals, and the soil.

      Each dandelion puff ball holds between 54 and 172 paragliding seeds which can be blown kilometers away from the parent plant even in mild winds.

      The hooks on the seed-shell can then catch on animal fur and be carried even further.

      If you have ever tried to rid your yard of dandelions, you know that, astonishingly, even the smallest bit of root left in the soil will regrow an entirely new plant!

      These are prime characteristics of an effective (r)evolutionary.
