    AKASHINGA

    The Akashinga Rangers are part of an innovative and effective programme that harnesses the power of the local community to combat poaching. Damien Mander — a former Australian special forces soldier — founded the Akashinga Rangers in 2017, a team of anti-poaching rangers made up entirely of women who stand up for African wildlife. As the rangers train and learn how to protect ecosystems, they become stewards of these landscapes and wildlife while developing personal and financial security and elevating their communities. Akashinga's strategic approach doesn’t simply provide guns to stop poachers. Instead, we call for community involvement and support. If communities could recognise the benefits of protecting and preserving animals and their habitats, poaching could die out without the need for armed confrontations. Akashinga embraces an innovative, effective approach to conservation that empowers women and communities while protecting wildlife and habitats.

    You can view Akashinga: the brave ones, a short film by National Geographic produced by James Cameron HERE.
