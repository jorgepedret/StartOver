      Take back your sound

      Learn to make sounds again...

      ... for no reason

      This experiment is best done in a group - like a Possibility Team with a spaceholder who 'runs' the space.

       

      1st part. The group (as well as the spaceholder) walks in the room and when meeting someone, start a conversation in gibberish. It is a regular conversation, but it totally uncomprehensible. When the conversation obviously comes to an end, keep walking about the room and start another conversation in gibberish and so on for about 5-6 min.

       

      2nd part. The group forms a circle.

      The spaceholder asks one person: "Give me a sound for this (not naming the thing just pointing at it - for example, the floor)" or "Give me a sound for dust (naming the thing)" "Give me a sound for the opposite of crystal". Spaceholders, let your own imagination cut loose.

      The person might stall.

      This is because we have learn to have to learn the right answer in school. And because there is no right answer here, the person does not know what is appropriate to say.

      The spaceholder should encourage them: "Just let the first sound that comes out." or "Just open your mouth and let something come out".

      If it is clear that the sound the person makes is not the first one that was coming out, the spaceholder stops and asks: "What was the first sound that wanted to come out?"

      Then move on to the next person and the next.

      Spaceholder, to bypass the mind trying to give the right answer. You have to ask the question faster than usual and in nonlinear way (not following the circle, etc..)
