        DESIGNING THE FLOW

        Since thousands of years in Bali, everyone knows that the family who farms the rice paddy at the bottom of the valley has the responsibility to design and direct the water flowing down the mountains so that everyone, including the bottom family, has enough water to grow rice for everyone. The Balinese value Whole System Flow of resources.

         

        The principle of Whole System Flow is also the BASIS OF ARCHEARCHAL ECONOMICS!!!!! The last people in the flow are the ones who design the cash path to make sure it goes through everyone's hands before it starts over! The cash goes through a full cycle thereby staying fresh and healthy.

         

        Modern culture, in comparison, values Miserly System Flow, where cash is coveted and stashed away as if it means something to have a lot of cash. In capitalist patriarchal empire economics, cash is trapped itself inside of a cash hierarchy that tries to maximize cash at the top of the hierarchy regardless of the scarcity of cash at the bottom of the hierarchy.

         

        Having too much money makes people lose their senses. Modern culture's hierarchical economy fails due to lack of sensibility. Collecting money at the top of a hierarchy is as nonsensical as it would be for people at the top of the Balinese mountains collecting water in stagnant ponds even though it means that crops at the bottom of the valleys die from lack of water.

         

        The idea of accruing capital and interest equates to stealing water from the flow of water through all the rice paddies and storing it in a private cistern.

         

        How sane is it to allow someone higher up the mountain to withdraw water into ponds thereby killing the rice paddies below them?

         

        The people at the base of the pyramid - or more appropriately, at the outer circle of the torus - will automatically assure that everyone before them in the flow has enough cashflow to thrive so that they themselves can also thrive. The people higher up the mountain care about the people below them on the mountain.

         

        This Balinese design is smart when applied to economics because the cash flows through everyone. Why not?

         

        If the people at the outer fringes of the social torus do not have enough cash-flow to provide their Archetypal Lineage services to the village - in the Balinese example this equates to growing rice - then the rest of the village loses.

         

        If anyone anywhere in the social network cannot deliver their Archetypal Lineage services to the village, the village becomes poor.

         

        The village thrives when all the people get to have a chance to thrive rather than merely survive.

         

        The ideal scene is if everyone has enough water to grow rice!

         

        The ideal scene is if everyone has enough cash to thrive!

         

        It is the same value: Whole System Flow!

         

        There is more than enough money everywhere around the Earth that every person (if we slow down our overpopulation...) could have enough money to thrive, and could spend their time and energy creating cool possibilities instead of sacrificing their time and energy as an economic slave to try to get enough money to merely survive.

         

        Even if people sat around smoking dope with their, it would be better than forcing them into the frantic insanity of waking up each morning terrified for their lives... don't you think?

         

        In archiarchy, the gameworlds located at the periphery of the economic torus make sure that money flows through everybody's bank account and not get stashed away in certain people's bank accounts because they think it means something.

         

        It does not harm money if small amounts flow rapidly through billons of people's bank accounts rather than having cash in the billions flowing slowly through a few people's bank accounts!
