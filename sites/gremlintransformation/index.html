        • Gremlin Transformation

          The point of transforming your Gremlin is to make what you receive available for others, in other words, to jack into the Flow.

          No one can transform your Gremlin for you.

          More interestingly, no one can stop you from Gremlin Transformation.

          After that, no one can stop you from providing Gremlin Transformation for others.

          Your next culture profession and Possibilitator Specialty might be Gremlin Transformer.

          The world is in need of Radically Responsible Possibilitators.

          Are you willing to become one?

          Next culture is powered by skillsets and values that modern culture knows nothing about.

           

          Gremlin Transformation is one of these skillsets.

           

          Gremlin Transformation is evolutionary. It moves you (and your Gremlin) into entirely new domains of relating ecstatically, of creating originally, and inventing responsibly.

           

          The distinctions, tools, and processes in Gremlin Transformation are central to ANY next-culture adulthood initiation Path.

          Without Gremlin Transformation and its associated initiatory processes, you will not have enough experiential clarity, free attention, adult matrix, or freedom of movement to be Present without your Gremlin automatically running your life. You will be reactive and only able to enact your automatic survival strategy.

           

          But you have already survived. Has the survival thing gone on long enough?

           

          Only you can decide.

           

          What lies beyond survival? Most people think the answer is: dying. But the surprising actual answer is: living.

           

          The untransformed Gremlin is dedicated to keeping you in survival.

           

          The transformed Gremlin is at your side as an ally - as an unlimited resource of nonlinear possibility - dedicated to you living full out.

        • Reports from Gremlin Training #1

          Report from WEEK 3: GREMLIN DIET

          Gremlin is a fascinating concept. So is Gremlin Training. So is the Gremlin Diet.

           

          Unfortunately, fascinating concepts don’t change anything. They ping around in the mind, but they don’t stir the water deeply enough to create authentic transformation. Authentic transformation requires practice and experimentation.

           

          After putting together their lists of 50 Gremlin foods, selecting 5 of these foods, and then choosing a day of the week as their Gremlin feeding day, the Gremlin Hunters of Gremlin Training entered the realm of practice and experimentation. For the first time in their lives, they went cold turkey on their Gremlin foods. They quit coffee, sleeping in, eating ice cream, creating Low Drama with their loved ones, stealing, procrastinating, being right, indulging in jealousy, scrolling phones, hooking people with sexual energy, objectifying men and women, lying, taking shortcuts at work, sending emails while driving, hitting the snooze button on their alarm clocks, gossiping, playing 15 games of online chess in a row, having pizza 3 times a week, and more. They kept a BEEP! Book at their side and jotted down what they noticed. They shared their struggles and successes in the Telegram Group. They did Emotional Healing Processes (EHPs). They met with their 3Cells and worked with what was coming up. And when they ate Gremlin food and it wasn’t their Gremlin Feeding Day, they didn’t beat themselves up (another Gremlin food). Instead, they started over.

           

          When Gremlin Training stays a theory, nothing changes. When Gremlin Training becomes a practice, the Liquid States are big and rapid. And where there are Liquid States, there’s also the possibility of change.

           

          Here are some of the things people reported this week:

           

          • “I'm starting to see how my Gremlin has taken over my life.”

          • “For the first time, I’m discovering that my Gremlin is my ally, not my enemy. He wants to serve me.”

          • “I always feel like an outsider in spaces like this. I am letting myself be more fully in this space and experiencing the love that is here.”

          • “My Gremlin’s role is to stop me from feeling pain. He keeps me cool and sarcastic. I’m seeing how he keeps me from feeling the pain that comes from the belief that I am not worthy of love. And I’m doing an Emotional Healing Process about it!”

          • “I am finally feeling the sadness of wanting so badly to connect and be in contact with people, and how my Gremlin sabotages it to protect me.”

           

          These are words from people at the edge of transformation.

          Report from WEEK 5: UNHOOKABILITY, NON-LINEARITY, (IN)SANITY, & HIGH-LEVEL FUN

          Pulling back the curtain and discovering the damaging, numbing, diminishing activities that the Gremlin is really up to leads to an experience that might broadly be referred to as "pain." Pain can include:

          • Remorse for the ways we have harmed the people around us through our unconsciousness...
          • Grief for the years that we avoided our true calling and instead stayed in jobs or relationships that were "safe" because we were scared...
          • Horror to discover that in every relationship where we assumed we'd been betrayed, wronged, door-matted, manipulated, or otherwise unfairly treated, we were the ones who set it up to be that way; we were the ones who were the perpetrators, victims, and rescuers...
          • Rage that we never knew we had a Gremlin because no one told us...
          • Shuddering sadness that the only barriers between us and real intimacy are the ones we've built ourselves...
          All of this "pain" might lead us to think (not unreasonably) that Gremlin is bad and that we should do whatever we can to get rid of him/her. But this attitude overlooks a really important slice of the Gremlin Pie: Namely, that Gremlin is an infinite resource of nonlinearity, creativity, nonsensical possibility, productive trespassing, etc., and, when properly trained, Gremlin can be one of our greatest allies in serving our relationships, our Archetypal Lineages, and the world.
           
          During Week 5, we dive into this world.
           
          We begin with Unhookability. Person A uses their Gremlin to toss a hook at Person B. Person B gets centered, grounded, and bubbled and then uses their Gremlin to swirl, spin, divert, rebound, Aikido, pirouette, or in some other manner refute the hook cast by Person A's Gremlin. It takes practice, but with some coaching and feedback, the Possibiltators start to look a little bit like Neo dodging bullets.
           
          Next up is nonlinearity. This process is little like speed dating. Two people meet in a room for two minutes inhabiting one identity. When the two minutes are up, the room closes, and it's onto the next room where each person inhabits a brand new identity. New voice, new faces, new posture, new topic. Two minutes pass, then onto the next room and the next identity. And so on and so on. When it's time to come back to the main room, people are aglow. Who am I? What's in an identity, anyway?
           
          Lastly, it's time for the 'Goin' Sane' process... But some things are better left to be experienced than explained...
